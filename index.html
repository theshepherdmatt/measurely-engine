Common questions

An impulse response WAV file exported from REW (Room EQ Wizard). The impulse response captures how your room responds to sound — it contains everything Measurely needs to calculate your six acoustic scores. To export one: run a measurement sweep in REW using your microphone, then go to File → Export → Export Impulse Response as WAV. REW is free to download from roomeqwizard.com and runs on Windows, Mac, and Linux. The exported file is typically a stereo WAV between 1–5 MB. Make sure you export the impulse response specifically — not the frequency response graph or the raw sweep recording. If you're unsure, the file should have a duration of a few seconds and open cleanly in any audio player.
REW is free and produces the most accurate results, so it's what we recommend — but it's not the only option. Any impulse response WAV at 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz will work, from other measurement software or a calibrated measurement microphone setup. Tools like HolmImpulse, FuzzMeasure (Mac), or AudioTools (iOS) can also export compatible files. The key requirements are: stereo WAV format, 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz sample rate, and a true impulse response rather than a raw sweep recording. If you're not sure whether your file is compatible, just upload it — Measurely detects the format immediately and will let you know if something doesn't look right.
Yes, completely free — no account, no credit card, no upload limit, and no catch. Measurely processes your WAV file entirely inside your browser using the Web Audio API. Your audio data never leaves your device; nothing is uploaded to any server. The analysis typically completes in under 5 seconds. You can create an optional account to save your session history across visits — but that's not required to get your full results. There is no paid tier that unlocks better scores or more detail. Every user gets the same complete analysis: six acoustic scores, a Co-Pilot commentary, and a ranked list of specific next steps for your room.
Yes, Measurely is tested and works on iOS Safari on both iPhone and iPad. Tap the upload button and select your impulse response WAV from the Files app, iCloud Drive, or any connected drive or cloud storage. On iPad, the full interface is available in landscape. On iPhone, the analysis and results work in both orientations, though the 3D room visualisation is best in landscape where there's more screen space. If you recorded your sweep on a Mac or PC and want to analyse it on your phone, transfer the WAV file to iCloud Drive and open Measurely in Safari — no app install required.
REW is a professional tool designed for acoustic engineers and AV installers. It produces detailed graphs — waterfall plots, spectrograms, impulse responses — that are extremely valuable if you know how to read them. Most home listeners don't, and that's not a failing; it's just a different audience. Measurely takes the same impulse response data that REW generates and translates it into six plain-English scores (Peaks & Dips, Reflections, Balance, Smoothness, Bandwidth, and a Co-Pilot commentary), each rated 1–10. You get a specific prioritised list of what to try first: move the sofa, add a panel here, toe-in the left speaker. No graphs, no jargon — just what your room actually needs.
Your measurement history is stored in your browser's local storage — it stays on your device and is only accessible to you. Nothing is sent to or stored on any external server. You can upload multiple measurements over time and track how each of your six scores changes as you adjust your room: move furniture, add acoustic treatment, try a different speaker position. Each session is saved with a timestamp so you can compare before and after. If you use multiple devices, history won't sync between them automatically, but you can run a new measurement on any device and get your current scores straight away.
