About Measurely

Built in the UK  ·  Matt

Measurely started in a workshop, not a boardroom. It was built to solve a specific problem: how do you know if the treble driver on a pair of vintage speakers is actually balanced — not by ear, but by data?

Where it came from

I restore vintage speakers — mostly Gale GS401s, a British design from the 1970s that I think still sounds remarkable when it's right. Customers bring them in and trust me to get the drivers balanced before they go back. For years I did that by ear. It worked, but it bothered me: ear is subjective, and two restores of the same model can sound meaningfully different depending on the day.

I wanted a way to run left and right channel sweeps and use the measurement data to make a clear call — treble up, treble down, leave it alone. Something that turned a graph into a decision. That's what Measurely started as: a workshop tool for one pair of speakers.

How it grew

Once the sweep-and-score workflow existed, it turned out to be useful for rooms too — not just drivers. The same idea applies: a listening room has peaks and dips, early reflections, imbalance between channels. REW can measure all of it, but most people who love music aren't acoustic engineers. They need scores, not spectrograms.

So Measurely grew from a speaker workshop tool into a room acoustics tool. The core idea stayed the same — take measurement data, translate it into plain English, tell you what to do about it. Six scores, a ranked list of next steps, a 3D view of what's happening in your room. Built for people who care about how their music sounds, not for people who want to read graphs for a living.

Who I am

I'm Matt. I'm a developer based in the UK, and I've been into sound — particularly vintage British hi-fi — for most of my adult life. Tinkering is the thread that connects most of what I do: taking something apart, understanding why it works, and making it better before putting it back together.

Measurely is a solo project. It's free because I wanted it to be genuinely useful, not because I'm building toward a paywall. The Measurely Remote kit is coming — a Raspberry Pi-based sweep device for rooms without a measurement mic — and that's how the project sustains itself.
